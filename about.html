About Tastes of Africa

We are on a mission to share the depth, diversity, and beauty of African culinary traditions with the world.

Our Mission

To celebrate, preserve, and promote the rich culinary heritage of Africa — one recipe, one story, and one meal at a time. We believe that food is the most powerful bridge between cultures, and African cuisine deserves its rightful place at the global table.

A Brief History of African Cuisine

African cuisine is among the oldest and most diverse in the world. With over 3,000 distinct ethnic groups across 54 countries, the continent's food traditions are as varied as its landscapes — from the spice-laden tagines of Morocco's ancient medinas to the fermented teff injera of Ethiopia's highlands, and the smoky jollof rice of West Africa's vibrant cities.

Long before European colonisation, African civilisations had sophisticated agricultural systems, trade networks, and culinary traditions. The ancient Egyptians cultivated wheat and barley along the Nile. The Swahili Coast traded spices with Arabia and India for over a millennium. West African kingdoms developed complex cuisines built on yam, sorghum, millet, and palm oil.

The transatlantic slave trade had a profound and often unacknowledged impact on global food culture. Enslaved Africans brought with them seeds, techniques, and knowledge that transformed the cuisines of the Americas — okra, black-eyed peas, rice cultivation, and the foundations of Southern American, Caribbean, and Brazilian cooking all trace their roots to Africa.

Global Influence

Today, African cuisine is experiencing a global renaissance. Chefs like Marcus Samuelsson, Nobu Adeyemi, and Fatmata Binta are bringing African flavours to Michelin-starred restaurants worldwide. The African diaspora has carried these traditions to every corner of the globe, and a new generation of food writers, chefs, and home cooks are reclaiming and celebrating their culinary heritage.

From jollof rice going viral on social media to berbere spice appearing on supermarket shelves in Europe and North America, African food is finally getting the global recognition it has always deserved.

Visit a Cultural Food Landmark

The Marché des Épices (Spice Market) in Marrakech, Morocco is one of Africa's most iconic culinary landmarks — a sensory journey through centuries of spice trade history.
