Culinary Regions of Africa

Africa's 54 countries span deserts, rainforests, coastlines, and highlands — each shaping a unique food culture.

West Africa

Bold, Hearty & Communal

West African cuisine is built on a foundation of palm oil, fermented locust beans (dawadawa), and an abundance of chillies. Meals are communal — shared from a single large bowl, eaten with the right hand.

Staple ingredients include yam, cassava, plantain, and a variety of leafy greens. The cuisine is known for its depth of flavour achieved through long, slow cooking and layering of spices.

Key Spices & Ingredients

  • Scotch bonnet peppers — fiery heat in most dishes
  • Iru / Dawadawa — fermented locust beans for umami depth
  • Crayfish — dried and ground for seasoning
  • Suya spice (yaji) — blend of groundnuts, ginger, and paprika

Signature Dishes

  • Jollof Rice — Nigeria & Ghana
  • Egusi Soup — Nigeria
  • Thiéboudienne — Senegal
  • Fufu with Light Soup — Ghana

North Africa

Aromatic, Spiced & Ancient

North African cuisine — spanning Morocco, Tunisia, Algeria, Libya, and Egypt — reflects thousands of years of trade along the Mediterranean and Saharan spice routes. The food is fragrant, complex, and deeply influenced by Berber, Arab, Ottoman, and Moorish traditions.

Couscous, the region's most iconic grain, was recognised by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2020.

Key Spices & Ingredients

  • Ras el hanout — a complex blend of up to 30 spices
  • Harissa — fiery chilli paste from Tunisia
  • Preserved lemons — fermented for intense citrus flavour
  • Saffron — the world's most precious spice
  • Cumin & coriander — the backbone of North African seasoning

Signature Dishes

  • Moroccan Tagine — Morocco
  • Couscous with Seven Vegetables — Morocco/Algeria
  • Shakshuka — Tunisia/Egypt
  • Koshari — Egypt

East Africa

Ancient Grains & Spice Trade Heritage

East African cuisine stretches from Ethiopia and Eritrea in the north to Tanzania and Mozambique in the south. The region's food reflects centuries of trade with Arabia, India, and Persia — particularly along the Swahili Coast.

Ethiopian cuisine is unique in its use of teff and the communal dining tradition of sharing from a single injera-lined platter. Coastal Swahili cuisine is rich with coconut milk, cardamom, and cloves.

Key Spices & Ingredients

  • Berbere — Ethiopia's signature spice blend with chilli, fenugreek, and korarima
  • Niter kibbeh — spiced clarified butter used in Ethiopian cooking
  • Cardamom & cloves — Swahili Coast influence from the spice trade
  • Coconut milk — essential in coastal Kenyan and Tanzanian dishes

Signature Dishes

  • Injera & Doro Wot — Ethiopia
  • Pilau Rice — Kenya/Tanzania
  • Nyama Choma — Kenya (grilled meat)
  • Ugali with Sukuma Wiki — East Africa
